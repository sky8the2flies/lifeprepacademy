FEDERAL GRANTS & LOANS

Federal student aid is designed to cover school expenses such as tuition, fees, room and board, books and supplies, and transportation. This financial assistance may be used to attend a four-year or two-year public or private educational institution, a career school, or a trade school. In order to receive this aid, you must:







Federal programs include:

• Federal Pell Grants for up to $6,345 are based on financial need. They are for up to 12 semesters for undergraduate students and do not have to be repaid.

• Federal Stafford Loans through a lender for first year undergraduate students in the amount of $5,500. If you qualify for a subsidized loan, the government will pay interest while you are in school. Otherwise, the interest must be repaid along with the loan if it is unsubsidized.

• Federal Plus Loans are unsubsidized loans made to parents.

• Campus-Based Programs which are administered by post secondary schools. They include Federal Supplemental Opportunity Grants from $100 to $4,000, Federal Work Study Programs allowing you to earn money while going to school.

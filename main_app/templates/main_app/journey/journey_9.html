CALIFORNIA'S PUBLIC COLLEGES AND UNIVERSITIES

In California, you have access to the largest network of colleges in the world.

In this section of the Guide, you will have the opportunity to learn about the California Community College System, transfer opportunities from a California community college to a California State University or UC Campus, the California State University System, the University of California System, independent universities, studying abroad, military schools, and distance learning.


SELECTION: Top 12.5% of high school graduates.

PROGRAMS: Undergraduate and graduate degree programs, including law, dental, pharmacy, and medical schools.

STUDENTS: Almost 240,000

CAMPUSES: 10

ESTIMATED COSTS: $29,500 for fees, room and board, books and supplies if living at home and commuting.

ENTRANCE REQUIREMENTS: Top 12.5% of high school graduates statewide or other criteria; 15 prescribed courses and appropriate admission tests.

PROGRAMS: Undergraduate and graduate degree programs.



STUDENTS: Over 460,000

CAMPUSES: 23

ESTIMATED COSTS: Approx. $17,500 for fees, room and board, books and supplies if living at home and commuting.

ENTRANCE REQUIREMENTS: Top third of high school graduates statewide, 15 prescribed a-g courses, and appropriate admission tests.


SELECTION: High school graduates or adults 18 or older.

PROGRAMS: Two-year associate degrees; vocational programs; certificate programs; transfer opportunities to UC or CSU.

STUDENTS: Over 2.5 million

CAMPUSES: 115

ESTIMATED COSTS: California residents currently pay $46 per unit for community college courses. See Califorina Promise.

ENTRANCE REQUIREMENTS: Open to all California residents 18 or older, including those without a high school diploma. Student may attend college anywhere in state.
